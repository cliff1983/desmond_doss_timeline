DESMOND DOSS

Heroic conscientious objector

Desmond Doss center with his U.S, Congressional Medal of Honor. (Desmond Doss Council)

Here is a timeline of Desmond Doss's life

  • February 7, 1919: born in to William Thomas Doss and Bertha Edward Doss
  • March 1941: Doss begin working as a ship joiner in Newport News Naval Ship yard.
  • April 1, 1942: Entered active duty
  • Late 1945: Was discharged from the Army
  • July 1944: His division took part in the liberation of Guam from the Japanesse
  • April 29, through May 26 1945: Dates of service
  • April 29 1945: begin fighting in Okinawa. Rescued 75 men who had come under withering, artillery, mortar and machine gun fire
  • 9May 2, 1945: Facing heavy machine gun fire doss rescued a wounded man 200 yards in front of the American lines.
  • May 4, 1945: He made four trips under heavy machine gun fire to treat and save four wounded man within 25 feet of a heavily defended Japanese cave.
  • November 12, 1945: Received Medal of Honor from president truman
  • 1950s: built a house in the town of Rising Fawn, in Northwestern Georgia
  • 1951: Training camp Desmond T. Doss, for young Seven Day Adventist about to enter military medical service was named in his honor.
  • 1959: Appeared on the television show ”This is your life”
  • 1990: A section of route 2 in Forte Oglethorpe, became Desmond T. Doss Medal of honor highway.
  • November 17, 1991: His first wife died following a car accident
  • July 1, 1993: Doss married, Frances May Duman a widow with three adult children.
  • 1998: Doss new wife wrote “In Gods Hand”
  • 2004: Terry L. Benedict, Completed a documentary film, “The Conscience Objector” about Doss.
  • May: 2007: A giant statue of Thomas depicting him saluting was unveiled at Veterans Memorial Park in Collegedale, Tennesee.
  • March 23, 2006: Desmond died at him home in Piedmont, Alabama of respiratory ailment.
